In regard to the conch-shell before our eyes, when it is found that the effective action resulting from its cognition is one that can be produced by the white object, it is understood that the cognition of the white conch-shell is not one produced by eyes affected by jaundice. The purity (perfection) of the cause having become thus recognised, if, at the same time, there should arise the cognition of the yellow colour in connection with the conch-shell made of gold,тАФas that also would have been brought about by flawless causes, there would be certainty regarding the validity of that cognition,тАФjust as in that of the cognition of the white colour in connection with the white conch-shell, which has led to effective action.тАФ(2991-2993)

Kamala┼Ы─лla

Says the Opponent:тАФтАЬThe Reason adduced in this argument is тАШinadmissibleтАЩ; because the тАШperfection of the causeтАЩ cannot be recognised without тАШconformity with the real state of the thingтАЩ; because the Sense-organs (which are the cause of Cognitions) are themselves beyond the reach of the senses; and when тАШconformity with the real state of thingsтАЩ is needed, that in itself constitutes well-ascertained validity; so that the recognition of тАШthe perfection of the causeтАЩ would serve no useful purpose; as it would come after the validity has become ascertained.тАФIt might be argued thatтАФтАШAt times, from the Cognition of the object close by, one would find that it is in conformity with effective action and from that he would know that the source of the Cognition has been perfect, and then he would conclude that the Cognition is valid; while at other times, in the case of the remote object, even though he may not be cognisant with the fact of its being in conformity with effective action, and yet, in course of time, he would become assured of the perfection of the source of the Cognition, and then secure certainty regarding its validityтАЩ,тАФBut this cannot be right. Because things being momentary and perishable, their activity cannot be always of one and the same form; because, as the result of the influence of a succession of causal conditions, they would be acquiring diverse potencies.тАЭ

Anticipating all this, the Author supplies the following answer:тАФ[see verses 2991-2993 above]

What is meant by this is as follows:тАФWe do not mean that, in the case of the Cognition of the remote object, appearing at some other time,тАФits validity is ascertained through the perfection of its source;тАФif this were our opinion, then alone could our Reason be тАШinadmissibleтАЩ, on account of the possibility of fresh potencies appearing under the influence of successive causal conditions;тАФwhat we do mean is that, in the case of the Cognition of the white conch-shell near us, at the same time that we have the certainty regarding the flawlessness of the cause brought about by the securing of the effect due to the white colour,тАФthere appears the Cognition of the yellow colour in the golden conch-shell lying far away from us,тАФit is concluded that as the perceptive cause has been found to be flawless, the said Cognition of the yellow colour must be valid, true.тАФIt is not possible that the source of Cognition should have become changed at the same time; as in that case, there would be no conformity of the Cognition of the proximate object with effective action.тАФ(2991-2993)
